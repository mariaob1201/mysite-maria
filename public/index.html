Maria Oros

Data Scientist & Statistical Researcher

I am a Data Scientist at the Data Science Institute at UW–Madison, where I develop advanced machine learning algorithms and statistical models under the supervision of Professor Kyle Cranmer. My work focuses on statistics and machine learning applications.

My research interests encompass causal inference, Bayesian statistics, and foundation models. I specialize in developing rigorous statistical frameworks that bridge theoretical advancements with practical applications, enabling data-driven decision-making across diverse domains.

Prior to joining UW–Madison, I majored in Mathematics through a joint program between the University of Guanajuato and the Center for Research in Mathematics (CIMAT), where I investigated Hamiltonian systems and their applications in mathematical physics.

I am passionate about building innovative solutions that drive scientific discovery and deliver measurable impact. I thrive in collaborative environments and have a proven track record of publishing research, delivering conference presentations, and contributing to open-source tools that advance the scientific community.
